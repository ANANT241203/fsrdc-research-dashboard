FSRDC Research Output Dashboard

An interactive visualization of U.S. Census FSRDC-linked research outputs

Project Summary

Dashboard Features:

  • Interactive bar chart of the top 10 RDCs by research output
  • Line chart showing publication trends over the years
  • Toggleable bar/line chart of the top 10 most prolific authors
  • 2D PCA projection plot visualizing research output groupings
  • Animated cluster plot (KMeans) showing how research clusters evolve over time
  • Searchable and filterable table preview of top research outputs
  • LDA topic modeling insights and word cloud visualization of research themes
  • Discrete Event Simulation (DES) conceptual insights on project lifecycle delays
  • Download link for the enriched dataset (CSV)
  • Dark mode/light mode toggle for accessibility
  • Team member and data/tool information sections

Key Results & Insights:

  • The Boston RDC leads in research output, followed by Michigan and Chicago
  • Publication activity has grown steadily since 2011, peaking in 2023
  • The most prolific author is David H Autor, with 46 outputs
  • PCA and clustering reveal three main research clusters, suggesting distinct research communities or institutional patterns
  • LDA topic modeling identified five major research themes: Health & Labor Economics, Firms & Innovation, High-Energy Physics, Gravitational Wave Science, and technical/markup artifacts
  • DES simulation suggests the median time from project start to publication is 3â€“4 years, with bottlenecks at approval and data access stages
  • The dashboard provides a comprehensive, interactive view of FSRDC-linked research, supporting further exploration and analysis

Top 10 RDCs by Research Output

The chart below highlights the top 10 Research Data Centers (RDCs) by the number of associated research outputs. This provides a broader view of research activity across more centers.

Publication Trend Over Years

This chart illustrates the growth in FSRDC-linked publications over time. There is a noticeable rise in output starting around 2011, with peak publication activity in 2023, suggesting growing utilization of restricted microdata.

Top 10 Authors

The chart shows the top 10 most prolific authors in the FSRDC ecosystem. This gives a more comprehensive look at researcher contributions.

PCA Projection

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) helps visualize the dataset in 2 dimensions based on year and project duration. This projection shows some natural grouping among research outputs.

Cluster Visualization

Using KMeans clustering on the PCA-transformed data reveals 3 major clusters of research outputs. These clusters may reflect temporal or institutional research behavior patterns.

Topic Modeling Insights

We applied Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to uncover latent research themes from publication titles. The model identified five prominent topics:

  • Health & Labor Economics: effects, health, labor, insurance, children
  • Firms & Innovation: firms, survey, entrepreneurship, policy, census
  • High-Energy Physics: proton, tev, collisions, measurement
  • Gravitational Wave Science: gravitational, wave, LIGO, performance
  • Markup/Formatting Noise: technical tags and math markup artifacts

Topic Modeling Word Cloud

This word cloud visualizes the most prominent terms identified through LDA topic modeling of research titles. Larger words appear more frequently across top topics.

Discrete Event Simulation (DES) Insights

To illustrate data flow in the FSRDC ecosystem, we implemented a conceptual Discrete Event Simulation (DES). The simulation models project lifecycles from proposal submission to publication, capturing delays from RDC approval, data access, and analysis.

  • Entities: Projects submitted to RDCs
  • Events: Proposal approval, data access granted, output published
  • Resources: RDC data specialists and reviewers
  • Key Insight: Median time from start to output is 3â€“4 years, with substantial queueing at approval and access stages.

This simulation highlights the importance of streamlining RDC processes to improve research throughput.
