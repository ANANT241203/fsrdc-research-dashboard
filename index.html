FSRDC Research Output Dashboard

An interactive visualization of U.S. Census FSRDC-linked research outputs, enriched and analyzed using advanced data science techniques. This dashboard reflects the full pipeline described in the project report, including data cleaning, enrichment, EDA, clustering, PCA, classification, text analysis, topic modeling, and discrete event simulation (DES).

Project Summary

Dashboard Features & Methodology:

  • Comprehensive data cleaning, deduplication, and enrichment (Crossref/OpenAlex APIs, project metadata join, robust error handling)
  • Interactive bar chart of the top 10 RDCs by research output, highlighting concentration in Boston and Michigan
  • Line chart showing publication trends over the years, with notes on data cutoff for 2024/2025
  • Toggleable bar/line chart of the top 10 most prolific authors, with insight on author name concentration
  • Co-authorship network insights, showing top author pairs and collaboration patterns
  • 2D PCA projection plot visualizing research output groupings (OutputType_x and metadata features)
  • Animated cluster plot (KMeans, k=3/4) and DBSCAN, with cluster interpretations (long-term journal projects, short-term drafts, specialized outputs)
  • Classification with SMOTE and Random Forest, dropping rare classes for robust modeling
  • TF-IDF KMeans text clustering and LDA topic modeling on OutputTitle, with thematic summaries and word cloud
  • DES simulation of project lifecycle, showing median 3â€“4 year time to publication and bottlenecks at approval/data access
  • Searchable and filterable table preview of top research outputs
  • Download link for the enriched dataset (CSV)
  • Dark mode/light mode toggle for accessibility
  • Team member and data/tool information sections

Key Results & Insights:

  • Top RDCs: Boston and Michigan lead in research output, with a sharp drop after the top 5 RDCs, indicating concentration in leading institutions.
  • Publication Trends: Steady growth since 2011, peaking in 2023; sharp drop in 2024/2025 likely due to data cutoff.
  • Authors: High concentration among a few prolific authors (notably J. Wang, Z. Wang, Y. Zhang), with common surnames suggesting research groups or teams.
  • Co-Authorship: Top pairs have 6â€“8 joint publications, indicating strong but distributed collaboration networks.
  • PCA & Clustering: Three main research clusters (long-term journal projects, short-term drafts, specialized outputs); DBSCAN isolates outliers and edge cases.
  • Classification: OutputType_x as target, rare classes dropped, SMOTE for balancing, Random Forest for robust multi-class prediction.
  • Text Analysis: TF-IDF KMeans and LDA reveal themes: housing/inequality, public health, labor markets, COVID-19, economic shocks, healthcare/rural policy.
  • DES: Median time from project start to publication is 3â€“4 years, with bottlenecks at approval and data access stages.
  • The dashboard provides a comprehensive, interactive view of FSRDC-linked research, supporting further exploration and analysis.

PCA & Clustering Visualizations

Explore different 2D projections and clusterings of the research outputs. Use the dropdowns to switch between PCA by OutputType_x, KMeans (3/4 clusters), DBSCAN, and TF-IDF KMeans text clusters. PCA did not reveal clear clusters by output type, but KMeans and DBSCAN uncovered meaningful groupings:

  • KMeans (k=3): Best separation (highest silhouette score). Clusters: (0) Long-term journal projects (NY/CA RDCs), (1) Short-term/draft projects (working papers/reports), (2) Specialized outputs (dissertations, region-specific).
  • KMeans (k=4): More granular, distinguishes in-progress reports and published articles.
  • DBSCAN: Isolates outliers and edge cases (unusual durations, page counts, or RDCs).

TF-IDF KMeans Text Clustering

Titles are grouped by semantic similarity using TF-IDF and KMeans (k=5). Example clusters:

  • Cluster A: Housing, income, urban inequality
  • Cluster B: Public health, rural access, healthcare policy
  • Cluster C: Labor markets, job mobility, economic shocks

Topic Modeling & Word Cloud

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was used to extract 5 major research themes from OutputTitle. Top topics:

  • Housing, inequality, and urban development
  • Gender gaps and STEM education outcomes
  • COVID-19 and small business impact
  • Economic shocks and unemployment
  • Healthcare access and rural policy
